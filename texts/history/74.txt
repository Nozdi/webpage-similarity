Bleeding Kansas

 The Kansas-Nebraska Act gave the settlers in the newly organized territories of Kansas and Nebraska the power to decide for themselves whether or not to permit slavery. This bill helped increase the debate over slavery. The increasingly bitter fighting between pro- and anti-slavery forces would cause the region to be named Bleeding Kansas.
