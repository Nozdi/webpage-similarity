The iPad didn't invent the tablet, but it did define it. And while the competition has played catch-up, the iPad spawned a juggernaut of an ecosystem, extending its abilities via thousands of accessories and hundreds of thousands of apps designed specifically for it.

The iPad doesn't have quite the "Wow!" factor as when it made its debut, but with each new release, it gets better and better. The media often comes away from release announcements with a certain amount of disappointment, expecting Apple to open the curtain to some big new feature, but that isn't the Apple way. Instead of adding various gadgets and gizmos such as IR blasters and Near-Field Communications, Apple adds subtle changes to the hardware and reveals new ways to compete with common technologies.
