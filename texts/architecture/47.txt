The New Tokyo City Hall Complex replaced the 1957 Tokyo Metropolitan Government Office, the first of a dozen government projects designed by Tange Associates. The new complex--two skyscrapers and an assembly hall--is dominated by the Tokyo City Hall Tower I skyscraper. Completed: 1991Architect: Kenzo TangeArchitectural Height: 798 1/2 feet (243.40 meters)Floors: 48Construction Materials: composite structureStyle: PostmodernDesign Idea: Two-towered Gothic cathedral, after Notre Dame in Paris The tops of the towers are irregularly shaped to lessen the effects of Tokyo winds.
