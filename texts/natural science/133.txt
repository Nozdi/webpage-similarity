The most abundant element in the body by mass is oxygen, from water

Question: What Are the Elements in the Human Body?

Answer: Most of the human body is made up of water, H O, with cells consisting of 65-90% water by weight. Therefore, it isn't surprising that most of a human body's mass is oxygen. Carbon, the basic unit for organic molecules, comes in second. 99% of the mass of the human body is made up of just six elements: oxygen, carbon, hydrogen, nitrogen, calcium, and phosphorus.
